# CaféMetrics — Business Insights Report
**Period:** April – June 2026 | **Prepared by:** Data Analysis Team

---

## 1. Executive Summary

The café generated **£124,373 in total revenue** across the April–June period, averaging **£1,382 per trading day**. Food is the strongest revenue driver at nearly half of all sales, while weekends consistently outperform weekdays by a meaningful margin. A few targeted changes — particularly around weekend staffing, food upselling, and mid-week promotions — could meaningfully lift revenue in the next quarter.

## 3. Revenue Trends

Weekly revenue settled into a **£9,000–£11,000 band** for most of the period, with a few standout weeks:

- **Week 24** was the strongest full week at **£11,082**, suggesting a mid-June uplift (potentially warmer weather driving food and drink sales).
- **Week 18** was the softest full week at **£8,240** — worth investigating for any local events, closures, or staffing issues that week.
- There is a **gradual upward drift** from week 15 onwards, indicating the café may benefit from spring/summer seasonality.

---

## 4. Product Performance

**Winners:**
- **Salad** leads all products on revenue (£26,835) despite a mid-range price point — customers are choosing it consistently.
- **Sandwich** follows closely at £23,025, confirming that hot/cold lunch items are the backbone of the business.
- **Smoothie** punches above its weight at £16,682, strong for a drinks item.

**Underperformers:**
- **Tea** (£9,006) and **Espresso** (£10,735) generate the least revenue. Tea in particular has low unit price and relatively modest volume — it may not justify the counter space it occupies compared to higher-margin drinks.

## 7. Actionable Recommendations

**1. Double down on weekends.**
Saturday and Sunday already outperform, but there is likely unmet demand. Consider extended hours, a weekend brunch special, or a loyalty stamp card redeemable on weekends to drive repeat visits.

**2. Run a Wednesday promotion.**
Wednesday is the weakest day by a clear margin. A simple mid-week offer — "2-for-1 pastries before noon" or a discounted lunch deal — could close the gap with stronger days without heavy cost.

**3. Grow the Food and Drinks mix.**
With Food at 48% of revenue and Coffee at only 31%, this café earns more from plates than cups. Introducing one or two new food or smoothie options (e.g. a seasonal salad, a new smoothie flavour) has a higher expected revenue impact than adding another coffee variant.
